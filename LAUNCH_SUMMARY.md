## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Don't:
1. **Over-promise** - Stick to what you can deliver
2. **Ignore critics** - Engage respectfully, even with skeptics
3. **Batch responses** - Engage in real-time (within 2 hours)
4. **Copy competitors** - Differentiate clearly
5. **Skip security** - It's your key differentiator
6. **Be overly promotional** - Value first, always
7. **Neglect community** - They're your growth engine
8. **Launch unprepared** - Test everything 3x before launch
9. **Ghost after launch** - Consistency is key
10. **Compromise on quality** - Better to delay than ship broken

## Contingency Plans

### If Waitlist is Slow (<1,000 by Week -1):

**Actions:**
1. Increase content frequency (daily threads vs 3x/week)
2. Add giveaway contest ($500 SOL for random waitlist signup)
3. Partner with 5 micro-influencers (pay for reach)
4. Post to more Reddit communities (broader reach)
5. Run Twitter/LinkedIn ads (small budget test: $500)

---

### If Launch Day Has Issues:

**Protocol:**
1. Immediate transparency (tweet acknowledgment)
2. Extend waitlist discount (+24 hours)
3. Offer affected users full refund + free tier upgrade
4. Document issue + resolution publicly
5. Follow-up with all users (personal apology)

---

### If Competitor Launches First:

**Strategy:**
1. Congratulate publicly (confident, not threatened)
2. Publish comparison content (honest differentiation)
3. Double down on security-first positioning
4. Highlight manual review (unique to OPTIK)
5. Community poll: "What features matter most to you?"
